Rules that make it work

  • Progressive overload: hit the top of the rep range on all sets → add 2.5–5 lb next session. This is the whole game.
  • Effort: stop each set with ~1–2 good reps left in the tank. Form holds on every rep.
  • Rest: 2–3 min on big compounds, 60–90 sec on isolation work.
  • Running long? Keep first compound → second compound → target isolation. Drop the last accessory, never the compounds.
  • Day 7 is rest. Walk, stretch, sleep. Growth happens on the recovery, not in the gym.

The two-store strategy: one quick weekly trip in town, one cheap bulk run when staples run low. Prices per 2026 Consumer Reports and store comparisons.

Walmart Supercenter YOUR WEEKLY STORE 660 S 1750 W, Springville · ~5 min

Everything on the list in one stop, including rotisserie chickens, whey, and creatine. Within ~3% of the cheapest store around, and it's in town — convenience is what keeps a bulk consistent. Order pickup in the app if you want to skip the aisles.

WinCo Foods MONTHLY BULK RUN 895 N 980 W, Orem · ~15 min · open 24 hrs

Cheapest grocer in the area (~3.3% under Walmart overall; chicken ~$0.44/lb and ground beef ~$0.40/lb cheaper). Hit the bulk bins for oats, trail mix, and pasta, and stock the freezer with meat once a month. Debit or cash only — no credit cards.

Costco (Orem) / Sam's Club (Provo) SKIP FOR NOW 648 E 800 S, Orem · 1313 S University Ave, Provo

Their $5 rotisserie chickens beat Walmart's ~$7, but the $50–65/yr membership doesn't pay for itself for one person buying this list. Revisit if you get a family membership or your food volume grows.

Smith's Marketplace FILL-INS ONLY Springville, in town

Closest option for forgot-something runs, but generally pricier than Walmart and WinCo for the same items.

Supplements: cheapest solid whey is Walmart's Equate (or Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard if you want the name brand — Walmart and Amazon carry both). Creatine: any plain creatine monohydrate — the cheap store-brand tub is chemically identical to the premium ones.

Recovery — the part that decides if this works

  • Sleep 8 hours, consistently. Your ring showed ~6 hrs in May — that actively fights muscle growth. Your recent ~7h49m is much better; protect it. Muscle is built while you sleep.
  • Priority order: (1) eating enough, (2) sleep, (3) training. That's also the order people neglect them.
  • Desk job: get a 10–15 min walk in daily — helps appetite, recovery, and keeps you from stiffening up.
  • Adjust by the scale: average your 3 weekly weigh-ins. Under +0.5 lb/wk for 2–3 weeks → +300 kcal. Over ~1 lb/wk and getting soft → −200 kcal.
  • At ~0.5 lb/week you'll reach 190 in roughly a year of mostly lean gains.
